Oyakodon Recipe

Oyakodon is a classic comfort food of Japanese home cooking.

Tender pieces of chicken, onion, and eggs, simmered in an umami-rich, dashi-based sauce and served over a bowl of fluffy steamed rice. Simple, delicious, comforting.

Ingredients

  • 120g onion
  • 285g boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 tbsp sake
  • 4 free-range eggs

For the dashi-based sauce

  • 120ml dashi
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 2 tsp sugar

For serving

  • 250g cooked japanese rice, per serving
  • 4 thinly-sliced spring onions
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 2 tsp sugar

Method

  1. Mix the dashi, soy sauce mirin and sugar until the sugar is dissolved.
  2. Slice the onion and finely chop the spring onion.
  3. Dice the chicken and marinate in the sake for five minutes.
  4. Crack the eggs into a bowl and gently whisk them, making sure not to combine the yolk and whites.
  5. With the stove off, add sliced onions to a medium frying pan in a single layer. Cover with the dashi-based sauce.
  6. Turn on the heat to medium and bring to a simmer. Once simmering, add the chicken on top of the onions.
  7. Once simmering again, lower the heat to medium-low. Cook, uncovered, for 5 minutes or until the chicken is no longer pink. Halfway through, flip the chicken.
  8. Increase the heat to medium. When the liquid starts to bubble, drizzle two-thirds of the eggs over the chicken and onions.
  9. As the eggs begin to set, add the rest of the eggs. Sprinkle the spring onion on top and cook on medium-low until the egg is done to your liking.
  10. Portion into two and serve atop rice. Drizzle the desired amount of sauce on top.
