Cinnamon Bun Recipe

This easy cinnamon bun recipe is a prime example that Swedes know how to live.

This recipe puts a lot of the cinnamon sugar on the top, rather than hidden away inside. Don’t be shy, really go for it.

Ingredients

For the cinnamon bun dough

  • 100g unsalted butter
  • 200ml milk
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 250g plain or wholemeal plain flour
  • 250g strong white flour
  • 1½ tsp fast-action yeast
  • 4 tbsp caster sugar
  • 2 free-range eggs
  • olive oil, for greasing

For the filling and topping

  • 75g unsalted butter
  • 100g caster sugar
  • 2 tbsp cinnamon
  • 1 free-range egg, beaten

Directions

  1. Heat the butter, milk and salt in a saucepan until the butter has melted. Allow to cool.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flours, yeast and sugar.
  3. Make a well in the centre and crack in the eggs. Pour in the lukewarm milk mixture and stir to form a sticky dough.
  4. Oil your work surface with 1 teaspoon of olive oil and knead the dough vigorously for 5-8 minutes. Don't be tempted to add flour as the dough will eventually become less sticky.
  5. Shape into a ball, and put into a large, greased bowl. Cover with a tea towel and allow to prove for an hour, or until doubled in size.
  6. Meanwhile, for the filling and topping, combine the softened butter with half the sugar and 1 tablespoon of cinnamon.
  7. Mix the remaining sugar and cinnamon in a separate bowl and set aside.
  8. On a floured work surface, roll out the risen dough into a 36x24cm rectangle. Evenly spread the cinnamon-sugar-butter.
  9. With the longest edge closest to you, roll the dough into a cylinder. Cut into 12 even slices.
  10. Place each slice onto a flattened paper cupcake case on a baking tray and cover with a clean teas towel to prove for 30-45 minutes, or until risen.
  11. Pre-heat the oven to 200C/180C Fan/Gas 6.
  12. Brush the tops of the buns with beaten egg and dust with the cinnamon sugar. Bake for 12 minutes until the bins are dark golden-brown.
